Design Philosophy

Design Goal

Solving problems, meet the users’ needs, enhances a product's usability

  • Understand user: define problems and provide solutions concisely
  • Enhances a product's usability: design principle including the scale, hierarchy, balance, contrast, repetition, rhythm, Pattern, white space, movement, variety, unity
  • Increase brand awareness: consistency of design elements, such as line, shape, color, grid, space, and Illustrations

Design Philosophy

    Simplicity is key “Less is More”

  • The design goal is to create simple, easy-to-follow designs that get people from where they are to where they want to be quickly, efficiently, and effectively.
  • A good design format that people can interface with and understand easily.
  • Thoughtful placement of design elements. Eliminate redundancy
  • Provide maximum information and function using a minimum number of components

Knowledge of Code

    Unity of Research + Design + Implementation

    "An architect would understand the properties of concrete, so why wouldn't a designer understand the properties of code?" Aarron Walter

    As a former architect and UI/UX designer who believe in the integration between design and build is very significant

    Writing code for my own website and still keep learning code will help me to implement design properly

Desired Working Environment

Creative & Collaborative

My goal is to make great design with great people. Looking for a place that has a creative and collaborative culture where sharing, helping and lifting others up is encouraged

Autonomy

Looking for a company that encourages me to think. Employees are empowered to innovate and work autonomously, without micro-managing or hand-holding.

Transparency

Workplaces with positive cultures support a philosophy of transparency so that every team member feels they know where they stand, where the company is headed, and in general they feel “in the loop."
