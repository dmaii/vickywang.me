DESIGN STRATEGY

to understand the problems of the goal and context of use of potential users I used some blockbusting activity and applied different technicals to better analyze the problem from different perspectives. I began by interviewing three people with 6 months, 18 months, and 36 months of horticultural experience.


Executive Intent

This project is all about creating a simple cooking process. user can easily add their pantry information into this app and generate receipts based on existing ingredients and personal preferences. In addition to that, they can also scan and visualize the pantry information in this app. Moreover, they can select ingredients and cuisine just the way you want it.

Target Audience

Gender: Male, Female, Transgender

Profession: Working and Non-working

Age Group: 16+

Working Professionals, those people who are willing to cook but have problems managing pantry and finding receipts

General Tasks

Users can add food to the pantry section, and choose the recipe style. They also can add cooking equipment to this application. If they create an account and follow some steps adding their cooking pieces of stuff, the app will create your favorite recipe.

USER FLOW

A User Flow is a route map for the user to navigate from the entry point to the end point in order to complete a task in the user interface. the user flow defines all navigation steps in the user journey in order to achieve their goal


CARD SORTING

Card sorting is a method used to help design or evaluate the information architecture of a site. In a card sorting session, participants organize topics into categories that make sense to them and they may also help me label these groups.


INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

information architecture is the practice of deciding how to arrange the parts of something to be understandable. Information architecture aims at organizing content so that users would easily adjust to the functionality of the product and could find everything they need without big effort.

USABILITY TESTING

Usability testing refers to evaluating a product or service by testing it with representative users. Typically, during a test, participants will try to complete typical tasks while observers watch, listen and takes notes. The goal is to identify any usability problems, collect qualitative and quantitative data and determine the participant's satisfaction with the product.
