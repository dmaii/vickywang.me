GARDENERS HUB


Gardeners hub helps the local community developing horticulture and friendship together

PROBLEMS

Gardeners Hub website aims to improve the horticultural experience by improving learning and sharing experience caused by a overwhelming of resources and misunderstanding that result in failure horticultural experience by those involved in the horticultural process. by offering more comprehensive social networking opportunities to the local community gardeners that result in reducing wasting resource and preventing establishment failure by improving learning and sharing experience.

PROCESS

The goal of this project is to find solutions and ideas to help gardeners to improve their horticultural experience and facilitate social network with neighbors.

Instead of shopping at the grocery store or shopping mall is more convenient to have house plants and fresh vegetables at home during this pandemic. Growing vegetables and house plants are more popular recently. Base on the necessity, this project focuses on developing a local social network that helps gardens sharing experiences and gardening plants/tools. The goal is to help all gardeners have a good horticulture experience.

USER RESEARCH

to understand the problems of the goal and context of use of potential users I used some blockbusting activity and applied different technicals to better analyze the problem from different perspectives.



Gardeners’ pain points

I began by interviewing three people with 6 months, 18 months, and 36 months of horticultural experience.


What is the major problem to plant their own food?

How they maintain the garden and use what method?


Pain points the gardeners mentioned. The primary takeaways from the gardeners were as follows:


Gardeners want to successfully get gardening information such as watering and seeding based on the local climate

Gardeners feel frustrated when advice is not helping them to solve the problems of the location or conditions of their garden.

Gardeners struggle to remember the complete tasks date.

New gardeners often made mistakes to hurt their plants such as overwatering or over prune based on the online instructions

There are overwhelming variables to grow plants for new gardeners

CONTEXTUAL INQUIRIES

I conducted four contextual inquiries by accompanying our target users on their site with 6 months, 12 months, and more than 18 months of horticultural experience. By conducting the contextual inquiries, I wanted to know more about:





Key Findings from the Contextual Inquiries


Finding 1

Gardeners want a variety of plants and horticultural tools. They would like to find friends nearby sharing plants and tools






Finding 2

New gardeners often made mistakes to hurt their plants. based on the overwhelming information from the internet to made poor judgment.






Finding 3

Gardeners become frustrated when advice is not applicable to the location or conditions of their garden. (local climate )






Finding 4

Gardeners struggle to remember the date of the complete task.
